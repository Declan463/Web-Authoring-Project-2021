History

The Ballyhoura Apple Farm started in Churchtown Co. Cork in 2006. It started with the planting of 1000 trees and was able to plant 1000 more in the spring of 2007. A further 2500 apple trees were planted in the spring of 2008. In 2014 the company purchased the old Molex factory in Kilfinane and they transported the entire orchard to Kilfinane and 95% of their trees survived the trip.

The company believes that new innovative food products with apples as a base has unlimited potential. They have a number of new innovative apple based products. The company started out from a market and sold juices on a stall. They then expanded their market to local butchers, Centras and Supervalues in the Limerick area. When they purchased the factory in Kilfinane they expanded their business nationwide. They now sell products to Supervalue, Centra, Aldi and Lidl nationwide. They have numerous tasting awards and best product awards.

The business was started by Maurice Gilbert in 2006 in his home town Churchtown but didn’t grow till local millionaire and business partner Gerry Murphy invested in the businesses in 2007. Now the business has a lot of investors and potential for high growth especially through the internet. The new investors have given the company the chance to grow all their own ingredients with the purchase of new lands. The company now grows its own beetroots, carrots, lemons and most recently have added cherry trees to their orchard.
